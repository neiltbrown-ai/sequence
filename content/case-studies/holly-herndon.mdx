<CbHeading>The Thesis: Build the Consent Infrastructure Before You Need It</CbHeading>

<CbDropCap>Holly Herndon deepfaked herself — on purpose. In 2021, the electronic musician and Stanford PhD created Holly+, a neural network trained on her own voice that lets anyone in the world upload a song and receive it sung back in her voice. One thousand songs were transformed in the first hour. Then she built a DAO — a decentralized autonomous organization — of hundreds of token holders who vote on which Holly+ creations can be commercially released. Approved songs are minted as NFTs. Proceeds split: 50% to the creator, 40% to the DAO, 10% to Herndon.</CbDropCap>

Then she built the infrastructure for everyone else. In 2022, Herndon and partner Mat Dryhurst co-founded Spawning — a startup "building the consent layer for AI." Their first tool, Have I Been Trained?, lets artists search 5.8 billion images in the LAION-5B dataset to see if their work is being used to train AI models. Two major AI companies — Stability AI and Hugging Face — agreed to respect opt-out requests through Spawning's API. The company raised $3 million.

<CbPullQuote>Herndon started working with AI voice models in 2016 — years before ChatGPT, Midjourney, or the mainstream AI debate. By the time the world panicked about AI and creative ownership, she had already built the governance model and the consent infrastructure.</CbPullQuote>

For the library, Herndon is the **AI-native ownership case** — the only creative in the inventory who is building the consent and governance infrastructure for the AI era. While other cases show how to capture value in existing structures, Herndon is building the structures themselves. Her financial profile is smaller than most cases in the inventory. Her significance is not in raw revenue — it is in structural innovation.

</CbSection>

Then build tools for everyone (Spawning, Have I Been Trained?). Institutional world validates the innovation (TIME 100 AI, Whitney Biennial, Serpentine). Validation creates policy influence (AI companies agree to opt-out). Infrastructure grows (Source+ marketplace, consent layer expands). And the cycle continues.

The hub is "Structural Foresight" — the ability to see structural disruption coming and build ownership frameworks before the disruption arrives. This is prognostic ability applied to infrastructure, not just creative work.

<CbSection id="lessons">

<CbHeading>Transferable Lessons</CbHeading>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Build Consent and Ownership Frameworks Now — Before You Need Them">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Herndon anticipated the AI compression years in advance. Any creative can start thinking about: who trains on my work? How do I opt in or out? What governance do I want over derivatives of my creative identity? Have I Been Trained? is a free tool that exists right now. Use it.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="Your Identity Is a Licensable Asset">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Holly+ proves that a creator's distinctive characteristics — voice, style, aesthetic — can be modeled, distributed, and governed as a platform. This applies beyond music: any distinctive creative voice could potentially become a licensable AI model. The question is not whether this will happen — it is whether you will own the model or someone else will.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="03" label="Proliferation Authenticates Originals">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Counterintuitive but critical: the more people use Holly+ to create derivative works, the more valuable the authenticated original becomes. This inverts the traditional scarcity model. Instead of protecting against copies, you encourage them — because copies increase the premium on the verified original. The framework calls this the authentication premium.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="04" label="Move from Art Practice to Infrastructure Building">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Herndon's arc — artist → AI collaborator → voice model → DAO → startup → consent infrastructure — is the most complete example of a creative building infrastructure that serves the entire creative economy. The highest-leverage position is building the rails, not just riding them. Spawning serves all artists, not just Herndon.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="05" label="What Wouldn't Transfer">
    <p className="cb-card-text">**Stanford PhD at CCRMA.** The specific academic credential at the specific intersection of computer music research and AI gives Herndon credibility in both art and technology worlds. This credential is rare. **First-mover timing.** Being at the AI/music intersection in 2016 gave years of head start. **Revenue vs. influence gap.** Herndon's structural innovations are extraordinary but her income is likely modest compared to most cases in the inventory. Influence and structural significance do not automatically convert to financial capture.</p>
    <p className="cb-card-text">**But the principles transfer universally.** Anticipate structural disruption. Build governance before you need it. Treat your creative identity as a licensable asset. Understand that proliferation can authenticate rather than dilute. Move from using tools to building them.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
