<CbHeading>The Thesis: The System, Not the Business</CbHeading>

<CbDropCap>In 2020, Buc-ee's told Chris Koerner no. He had approached the beloved Texas gas station chain — known for its massive stores, immaculate bathrooms, and cult-status snacks — about becoming the first authorized online retailer of their products. They disagreed. So he went ahead and launched it without them. He and his cousin Kirk Salisbury bought one of everything from a Buc-ee's store, photographed it, listed it on Shopify, and started selling. Texas Snax now generates over $400,000 per month.</CbDropCap>

He told this story publicly. Then he launched another company. Then another. Then he started a podcast to document all of it. The content reached 50 to 130 million people per month. Which attracted investors for his real estate fund. Which funded more parks. Which generated more stories. Which grew the content platform.

This is not a story about a business. It is a story about a system — and the creative discernment it took to design it.

<CbPullQuote>Koerner does not run a portfolio of businesses. He runs a system designed to operate at every stage of value capture simultaneously. The content builds the investor trust. The investors fund the parks. The parks generate the stories. The stories grow the audience.</CbPullQuote>

He applied three structures — a holding company model, a creator-as-platform content operation, and an operating partner model — each reinforcing the others. Together, these structures turned market discernment into a self-feeding machine.

</CbSection>

<CbHeading>Holding Company: The Machine That Builds Machines</CbHeading>

CoFounders.com formalizes the model Koerner had been running informally for years. He and Nik Hulewsky — his missionary companion in Hungary, now closest business partner — built a holding company and startup incubator that funds students with a salary, training, and a business idea, then builds the company alongside them as equity co-founders.

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Platform Multiplies Portfolio Value">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The holding company model's core advantage: **the platform multiplies the value of each portfolio company in ways that cannot be captured by the company alone.** When Koerner acquires Mining Syndicate, he applies his content distribution to fill hosting capacity. When he launches Fast Tree Care, he does not need Google ads. When he raises capital for parks, he does not need a placement agent. The platform does the distribution for all of it.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="The CoFounders Model">
    <p className="cb-card-text">CoFounders provides capital and infrastructure. Student co-founders provide labor and operational management. Equity is split according to contribution. The holdco retains ownership across the portfolio. **Over 1,100 students apply each month. The acceptance rate is under 1%.** The $65K domain signals a serious, established operation — not a side project — which drives the quality of the applicant pool.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="03" label="The Texas Snax Thesis">
    <p className="cb-card-text">14 million people per month search for Buc-ee's on Google. None of them could buy anything online. That was a market gap, not a brand choice. Texas Snax now ranks first organically for hundreds of related search terms. **Koerner runs it on approximately five hours per week.** The creative insight was not sell snacks online. It was: become the supply for a market with 14 million monthly searches and zero suppliers, and the SEO flywheel runs forever.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>

<CbHeading>Content Platform: The Moat Underneath Everything</CbHeading>

The most important structural decision Koerner made was treating his content as owned infrastructure rather than promotional activity. The average operator uses social media to announce products. Koerner uses it to build the distribution layer that makes every business launch cheaper, every capital raise faster, and every operating partner recruitment easier — simultaneously.

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Content Is Not Marketing. It Is the Moat.">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The content does not drive revenue directly. It eliminates the cost of customer acquisition, investor outreach, and talent recruitment across every business simultaneously. **That structural advantage compounds over time and cannot be replicated by anyone who starts building it later.** The content is the most valuable business asset in the portfolio — not any individual company.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="The MHP Guy Origin">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Koerner started an anonymous Twitter account to document what he was learning about mobile home parks. Anonymous because he did not know yet whether it would work. **Two things happened simultaneously: the content built credibility in the exact audience he needed for real estate capital raises.** When he posted deal economics, he was reaching people who could invest alongside him. The content was not marketing for a separate business. The content was the business development.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="03" label="Transparent Underwriting as Content">
    <p className="cb-card-text">When Koerner posts the full underwriting of an RV park acquisition — cap rate, debt terms, expansion plan, targeted IRR — he is converting deal transparency into investor trust at scale. **By the time he raises $6.5M for a specific park, the people writing checks have been watching him underwrite deals for two years.** The content is the due diligence. A traditional GP builds LP relationships through private meetings. Koerner builds them in public.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>

<CbPullQuote>The content platform is not marketing. It is the most efficient capital-raising infrastructure available to a non-institutional investor. He saw that before most operators did — and built it before he needed it.</CbPullQuote>

<CbHeading>Operating Partners: Separating Judgment from Execution</CbHeading>

The creative economy's core constraint is that the practitioner can only be in one place. Koerner solved this by separating judgment from operations: he provides the idea, the capital, the systems, and the platform advantages. The operating partner provides the daily management.

/>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Judgment Is the Scarce Resource, Not Time">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Koerner's judgment — about which ideas have legs, which markets are mispriced, how to structure deals — is the scarce resource. **His time is not the scarce resource.** The operating partner model deploys the scarce resource (judgment) at scale while outsourcing daily operations to people who are better at it. Fast Tree Care runs without Koerner touching a chainsaw. Texas Snax runs without him managing inventory.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="The Nik Hulewsky Partnership">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Koerner's primary business partner is someone he spent two years with as a missionary in Hungary at ages 19-21, knocking on 20,000+ doors with a 99.9% rejection rate. **The trust that produces is not something manufactured from a LinkedIn connection.** The depth of the partnership — and the division of labor it enables — is a function of a relationship that predates the business by 20 years.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>

<CbHeading>The Compounding Effect: A System, Not a Portfolio</CbHeading>

Koerner's system does not progress through stages — it runs all four at once. Individual company launches are Stage 1. Operating partner relationships are Stage 2. Equity positions are Stage 3. The GP carry and holdco are Stage 4. The content platform is what makes the whole system legible and holds it together.

<CbPullQuote>This is what it looks like when discernment is applied to markets instead of canvases. The creative act is the market read, not the product. The financial capture is the equity. The content is the moat.</CbPullQuote>

The honest caveat: some of Koerner's returns are skill; some are timing. The 2021 Bitcoin bull market made Mining Syndicate's revenue ramp possible at the reported magnitude. The 2019-2022 window for mobile home parks was a specific moment of institutional under-attention. He would likely agree. What is not timing-dependent is the system design itself — the insight that a content platform in a specific niche is the most efficient distribution infrastructure available to a non-institutional operator.

<CbSection id="lessons">

<CbHeading>Transferable Lessons</CbHeading>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="The Creative Act Is the Market Read, Not the Product">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Koerner is not creative in the sense of making things. He is creative in the sense that his diagnostic reads on market structure are original and non-obvious. **14 million monthly searches, zero supply — become the supply. An existing operation plus my distribution equals asymmetric return.** These are not obvious reads. They are what produces 10x outcomes from otherwise conventional operational foundations.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="Build the Content Platform Before You Need It">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The most powerful asset in the portfolio is not a company. It is the distribution infrastructure — 820K followers, 50-130M monthly views — that makes every subsequent launch cheaper. **That platform was built before most current portfolio companies existed. Before the real estate fund existed.** The platform did not follow the businesses. The businesses benefited from the platform.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="03" label="Separate Your Judgment from the Execution">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The operating partner model deploys the scarce resource (judgment) at scale while outsourcing daily operations. **Find your version of James (Fast Tree Care) and Kirk (Texas Snax).** Your time goes to the highest-leverage activity: identifying the next market gap, structuring the next deal, generating the content that funds all of it.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="04" label="Publish the Failures. They Validate the Wins.">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Most operators hide their failures. Koerner publishes them. The McAfee crypto crash. The partnership disputes. The businesses wound down. **The transparency is what makes the wins credible. And the credibility is what converts followers into LP investors, applicants, and customers.** The instinct to protect your narrative from failure stories is understandable and strategically wrong.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="05" label="Treat Deal Economics as Content">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Koerner publishes full underwriting of real estate deals — cap rates, IRRs, equity waterfalls, debt terms. Not as pitches. As content. **The people who find it interesting enough to follow are exactly the accredited investors he needs.** The content self-selects for the right audience. By the time he needs to raise capital, the work is already done.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>
