<CbSection id="thesis">

<CbHeading>The Thesis: The Tools Are the Commodity</CbHeading>

<CbDropCap>Everyone with a browser has Runway ML. Everyone with a browser has Sora. Paul Trillo has those tools too. So does every advertising director who read about them in a trade publication and spent a weekend experimenting. The tools are not the advantage. The tools are the commodity.</CbDropCap>

What Trillo has that the advertising director with a weekend of experiments does not: ten-plus years of understanding how moving images work emotionally. How pacing creates dread or wonder. How the gap between what the eye expects and what it sees produces meaning. How the uncanny registers differently from the merely strange.

AI video generation tools did not give Trillo those things. He had them before the tools existed. What the tools did was eliminate the execution gap between what he could see in his mind and what he could put on a screen — and in doing so, **made his ability to see the thing worth seeing more valuable, not less.**

<CbPullQuote>This is the Discernment Premium in real time: a technological shift commoditizes execution, reveals vision as the scarce resource, and concentrates advantage in the people who had the vision before the technology arrived.</CbPullQuote>

He applied three structures — an AI-augmented studio model, premium first-mover positioning, and an advisory practice — each building on prior depth. This case is documented honestly as early-stage. The structures are forming, not established. That is precisely what makes it valuable: it shows the transition while it is happening.

</CbSection>

<CbHeading>AI-Augmented Studio: Judgment Directing the Machine</CbHeading>

The gap between Trillo's early AI films and the output of every other advertising director who tried the same tools is not a gap in tool access. Both groups had Runway. Both groups had time to experiment. The gap is in what each person brought to the tools when they sat down.

<CbSubheading>What Prior Depth Produces</CbSubheading>

/>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="The Tools Gave Everyone the Same Paintbrush">
    <p className="cb-card-text">AI video generation in 2022 was technically limited, aesthetically rough, dismissed by most professional filmmakers as a toy. Trillo saw something in it anyway. Not what the tools could do in 2022, but what they were directionally moving toward. **He started making work that was distinctive not because the tools were powerful — they were not yet — but because he was using imperfect tools to make images that were interesting.** The imperfection was sometimes part of the work.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="Early Adoption Without Depth Produces Noise">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The creative internet in 2022 and 2023 was full of AI-generated video. Most of it was indistinguishable — technically varied, interesting as demonstration but not as art or commercial communication. **Trillo's early work was distinguishable because it came from someone who had spent a decade learning to distinguish good from not-good in moving images.** The tools gave everyone the same paintbrush. His training gave him the eye.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>

<CbPullQuote>AI commoditized the execution of moving images. It made Trillo's discernment more scarce, not less. This is the Discernment Premium operating on a new input — and it proves the thesis that vision, not tool access, is what produces asymmetric outcomes.</CbPullQuote>

</CbSection>

<CbHeading>Premium Positioning: The Dual Legitimation</CbHeading>

In 2023, two things happened that documented the transition publicly: Trillo's work appeared on the cover of WIRED, and a piece was exhibited at MoMA. These are not equivalent validations — WIRED is journalism, MoMA is institutional art — but together they created a position that neither alone could have.

<CbMetrics>
  <CbMetric value="WIRED" label="Cover — Technology Press" />
  <CbMetric value="MoMA" label="Exhibition — Institutional Art" />
  <CbMetric value="2023" label="Same Year, Different Credibility Systems" />
</CbMetrics>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Dual Validation Compounds">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The WIRED cover documented first-mover status in the context of technology's cultural moment. The MoMA exhibition validated the work in the context of art. **Together, they spoke to both brands (who read WIRED) and institutions (who read MoMA's choices as signals about what matters).** Neither validation was available to the advertising director who spent a weekend with Runway. They required work specific and compelling enough to earn recognition on its own terms.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="The First-Mover Premium Has a Half-Life">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Trillo's current premium is generated partly by scarcity: few people have his combination of prior filmmaking depth and genuine AI-native practice. **That scarcity will compress over time as more filmmakers develop serious AI practices.** How Trillo builds ownership during this window — IP, platform, structures that persist beyond the first-mover premium — is the open question in the case.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>

<CbSubheading>The Shift in Commission Structure</CbSubheading>

Pre-AI, brands hired Trillo to execute their creative concepts. Post-AI legitimation, brands began hiring him because they wanted **his creative vision applied to their problems** — with AI as the medium. The distinction is the Stage 2 transition made visible: clients stopped asking *can you execute this?* and started asking *what should we make?*

</CbSection>

<CbHeading>Advisory Model: Judgment at Institutional Scale</CbHeading>

Beyond brand commissions, Trillo has been developing positioning at the intersection of art, technology, and AI policy — speaking at conferences, advising organizations navigating AI's impact on creative work, and building a creator platform that documents his process.

<CbTable
  headersJson='["Stream","Description","Status"]'
  rowsJson='[["Brand commissions","AI-native creative direction; clients seeking specific vision","Active; primary revenue"],["Advisory","Organizations navigating AI-creative intersection","Active; secondary revenue"],["Speaking","Conferences; AI and creative industry events","Active"],["Creator platform","Documenting AI filmmaking process; building owned audience","Early stage; growing"],["Future IP","Potential: courses, workshops, direct audience products","Not yet established"]]'
/>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Thought Leadership vs. Brand Commissions">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Brand commissions are transactional — project-based, with an end date. Advisory and thought leadership positioning builds cumulative standing. **The advisory work establishes Trillo as the person institutions turn to when they need to understand what AI is actually doing to creative practice — from the inside, not from the trade press.** That standing compounds in ways project work does not.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="The Creator Platform as Emerging Asset">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The creator platform — documenting the AI filmmaking process as it develops — is not just audience building. **It is historical documentation of a practice at the frontier.** The WIRED cover and MoMA documented a moment; the platform documents the ongoing work. Both forms compound over time. The practitioner who works at the frontier without documenting leaves no record for credibility to attach to.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>

<CbPullQuote>The first-mover premium has a half-life. The structures that persist — IP ownership, platform audience, institutional relationships, advisory retainers — need to be established while the leverage is highest. Waiting until the category matures means building from a less advantaged position.</CbPullQuote>

This case is valuable precisely because it is incomplete. It shows what the transition looks like while it is happening, rather than retrospectively. The open questions — what ownership structures get built during the first-mover window, whether the creator platform develops into significant owned audience — are the questions the creative majority faces as AI tools mature.

<CbSection id="lessons">

<CbHeading>Transferable Lessons</CbHeading>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Ask What You Already Know That AI Can Amplify">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Trillo's AI films are good because his prior decade of directing is good, amplified by tools that remove the execution gap. **The practitioners who capture the Discernment Premium in AI-augmented work are those who bring genuine prior depth to the tools — not those who learn the tools and hope the depth follows.** Identify what you already know deeply. Then find the tools that amplify that knowledge.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="Adopt Significant Tools Before the Category Is Crowded">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The window between when a transformative tool becomes available and when it becomes crowded is the first-mover window. **Trillo's 2022 adoption was in that window for AI video.** The window is narrow and moves fast. Paying attention to what is coming — not what has arrived — is the practice that produces first-mover positioning.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="03" label="Document the Practice in Public">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The creator platform — documenting AI filmmaking as it develops — is not just audience building. It is historical documentation of a practice at the frontier. **The practitioner who works at the frontier without documenting leaves no record for credibility to attach to.** The WIRED cover documented a moment. The platform documents the ongoing work. Both compound over time.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="04" label="Build Ownership Structures During the First-Mover Window">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The premium Trillo commands now is partly first-mover scarcity. That scarcity will compress. **The structures that persist — IP, owned audience, institutional relationships, advisory retainers — need to be established while the leverage is highest.** Waiting until the category matures to build ownership means building from a less advantaged position.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="05" label="The AI Transition Is Not About the Tools">
    <p className="cb-card-text">The tools are the commodity. Everyone has them. **The transition is about having the judgment to use tools well before most people understood what well meant in this medium.** That judgment — built through a decade of directing, not through a weekend of prompting — is the scarce resource. AI commoditized execution. It made discernment more scarce, not less.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>
