<CbSection id="thesis">

<CbHeading>The Thesis: Sometimes the Most Strategic Move Is Trading Income for Ownership</CbHeading>

<CbDropCap>In 2015, Mimi Chao walked away from a corporate law career at Latham & Watkins — one of the highest-grossing law firms in the world — to draw pictures. A decade later, she owns a studio with three independently published picture books carried in over 100 boutiques worldwide, runs her own creative education platform with mindfulness-integrated curriculum, teaches as a Skillshare Top Teacher to 85,000+ students, and sells art products direct to a loyal audience that has been buying her calendars since 2016. She almost certainly earns less than she did as a lawyer.</CbDropCap>

That is the point. Every other case study in this library tracks upward income trajectories. Chao is the first where the subject probably earns less than their pre-creative career — and that trade was deliberate. She owns 100% of a growing IP portfolio, a community, a product business, and a reputation. The attorney path generated zero transferable equity. The creative path generates assets that compound.

<CbPullQuote>The financial comparison only makes sense on a 20–30 year horizon, not a 10-year one. If Chao had stayed at Latham for 14 years, she would be earning $500K–$1M+ as a partner with $3–6M in career earnings — and zero transferable equity. Instead, she owns every ISBN, every character, every course, every wholesale relationship. This is the long game.</CbPullQuote>

For the library, Chao is the **practice business case** — the creator who builds a studio optimized for creative satisfaction and life integration rather than scale. She is also the **IP-first case**: rather than building a services career and then pivoting to products, she started creating owned IP within three years of leaving law. And she is the most relatable case for the core audience who wonders: "Can I really leave a good salary for this?"

</CbSection>

Teach the practice — not just the skill (85K+ students, mindfulness integration). Build owned platform (Mimochai Studio, reducing Skillshare dependency). <CbSection id="lessons">

<CbHeading>Transferable Lessons</CbHeading>

<CbAccordion>
  <CbAccordionCard num="01" label="Build Owned IP Alongside Client Work — From Day One">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Do not wait until you can afford to. Chao self-published within three years of leaving law while still taking freelance commissions from Disney, Adobe, and Samsung. The client work funded the IP work. Every year of delayed IP creation is a year of lost compounding.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="02" label="Use a Platform Year Strategically">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Take a time-bounded operational role at a company in your target industry — learn distribution, team management, product development, and character IP at someone else's risk. Then leave with the knowledge. Chao's Cubcoats year taught her everything she needed to run a product business. This is different from staying indefinitely in corporate work: it is deliberate, bounded, educational.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="03" label="Migrate to Owned Infrastructure Before You Need To">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Chao launched Mimochai Studio while Skillshare was still performing well for her — not after algorithm changes forced her hand. Discovery platforms (Skillshare, Instagram, Faire) bring audiences. Owned platforms (your site, your membership, your email list) retain them. Build the bridge before the flood.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="04" label="Define Enough Before the Market Defines It for You">
    <p className="cb-card-text">Three books in ten years. Twelve classes. A small team. No rapid scaling. No venture capital. This is not a growth business — it is a practice business. Chao's model is closest to Craig Mod's "enough" philosophy: build a business that sustains creative life rather than one that maximizes revenue. Know your number.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
  <CbAccordionCard num="05" label="What Wouldn't Transfer">
    <p className="cb-card-text">**BigLaw savings as runway.** Four years at Latham likely provided $100K–$300K in savings — a cushion most creatives do not have. **Instagram timing.** Her organic growth happened during a specific platform window (2015–2019) that no longer exists. **The cultural narrative.** "Lawyer leaves BigLaw to draw" generates press and speaking invitations — a one-time narrative advantage. **LA as creative market.** Access to Disney, craft fairs, and the children's product ecosystem.</p>
    <p className="cb-card-text">**But the IP-first practice business model transfers completely.** Build owned IP alongside client work from day one. Use strategic platform years for operational education. Migrate to owned infrastructure before you need to. Define "enough" before the market does.</p>
  </CbAccordionCard>
</CbAccordion>
