<SbSection id="overview">

<SbHeading>Overview</SbHeading>

The Royalty + Equity Hybrid pays you two ways: **ongoing per-unit royalties** (3–12% of sales, generating immediate recurring cash flow) plus a **small equity stake** (0.5–5%, capturing long-term appreciation from company growth or exit). Instead of choosing between "money now" (royalties) or "money later" (equity), you receive both — building a diversified compensation portfolio where royalties fund your life today while equity builds wealth for the future.

The principle: royalties and equity serve different financial purposes. Royalties generate income from day one of sales, providing cash to live on. Equity generates wealth over 3–10 years through company appreciation, potentially worth millions at exit even if individual royalty payments are modest. Combined, they create asymmetric advantage: if product sales explode, both components pay handsomely. If sales are moderate but the company gets acquired, equity compensates. If the company stalls but product sells, royalties compensate. You're protected from multiple downside scenarios simultaneously.

Product designers receive 5–10% wholesale royalty plus 2–3% equity when partnering with manufacturers. Musicians license masters for per-stream royalties plus equity in the platform. Authors negotiate traditional royalties plus equity in the company adapting their IP. The structure works wherever your creative contribution drives both product sales (royalty) and company value (equity).

<SbSection id="protect">

<SbHeading>Key Protections</SbHeading>

Five provisions covering both the royalty and equity components of hybrid deals.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="Gross Royalty Definition (No Net Deductions)">
    <SbCardText>"Royalty base is gross wholesale price — before ANY deductions. No deductions for manufacturing, marketing, distribution, overhead, returns (beyond 15%), discounts, or other costs." Net royalties on hybrid deals are doubly problematic: if royalties are suppressed AND equity fails, you earn nothing.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** The royalty component is your downside protection — if equity is worthless, royalties must be real. Net royalty definitions that reduce your 7% gross to effectively 2% defeat the entire purpose of diversified compensation.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Anti-Dilution Protection on Equity">
    <SbCardText>"Weighted average anti-dilution protection. If Company issues equity below Artist's price per share, Artist's conversion adjusts to maintain closer to original percentage. Pro-rata participation rights: Artist may invest to maintain ownership percentage in future rounds."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Without anti-dilution, your 3% equity dilutes to 1.5% after Series A, 0.8% after Series B, 0.4% after Series C. By exit, your "3% equity" is 0.4% — worth 87% less than promised. Anti-dilution + pro-rata rights preserve your ownership economics.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Vesting Acceleration on Change of Control">
    <SbCardText>"Upon Change of Control (acquisition, merger, or asset sale), 100% of unvested equity immediately vests. Artist receives full equity value regardless of time elapsed in vesting schedule." Without this, acquisition at Year 2 of 4-year vest forfeits 50% of your equity.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** The entire point of equity is exit-event payoff. If the exit happens early and half your equity is unvested, you lose the upside you negotiated for. Single-trigger acceleration ensures you capture full value when value is created.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Royalty Audit Rights with Fee Recovery">
    <SbCardText>"Artist may audit Company books annually upon 30 days notice. If underpayment exceeds 5%, Company pays underpayment plus 8% annual interest plus audit costs up to $15K." Pair with quarterly reporting: itemized by product, channel, territory.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Royalty reporting is your primary transparency mechanism. Without audit rights, you trust the company's self-reported numbers. Fee recovery creates deterrent: honest reporting costs nothing; dishonest reporting costs the audit plus interest.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Negotiate Components Separately (Not Bundled)">
    <SbCardText>**Process:** Establish fair royalty rate first (benchmark against industry standards). THEN negotiate equity independently. Never accept "5% royalty + 1% equity as a package" — the bundle obscures whether either component is fair. Compare each to its standalone market rate.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Companies present bad royalty + bad equity as "balanced hybrid." 3% net royalty (market: 8% gross) + 0.5% equity (market: 2–3%) is below-market on BOTH components. Negotiating separately exposes whether each is independently fair.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

<SbSection id="manipulations">

<SbHeading>Common Manipulations</SbHeading>

Four tactics that exploit hybrid complexity to underpay on both components simultaneously.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="'Equity in Lieu of Royalty' Pressure" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "We'll give you 4% equity — that's better than $50K/year in royalties!" Company avoids current cash payments by substituting speculative future value. Your 4% dilutes to 1.5% post-funding. If company fails (70–80% odds), equity = $0. You traded guaranteed income for nothing.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Equity doesn't replace royalties — they serve different purposes. Royalties fund your life now. Equity builds wealth later. Never accept "either/or" when "both" is appropriate for your contribution level.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Equity doesn't replace royalties — they serve different purposes. I need current income from royalties AND long-term upside from equity. I'll accept 4% equity if paired with 5–7% royalty. Or if you can't do royalties, equity needs to be 8–10% to compensate for the income I'm forgoing.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Bad Terms on BOTH Components" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "3% net royalty + 0.5% equity — a hybrid deal!" Both below market. 3% net = ~1% gross (market: 6–10%). 0.5% equity dilutes to 0.2% (market: 2–3%). Combined value: ~10% of what a fair single-component deal would deliver. "Hybrid" label masks terrible terms.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** A bad hybrid is worse than a good single component. 8% gross royalty alone outperforms "3% net + 0.5% equity." Each component must independently meet or approach market standards.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Let me benchmark both components separately. Your 3% net royalty equals ~1% of gross — market rate is 6–10%. Your 0.5% equity dilutes to 0.2% — market is 2–3%. Both are well below market. I need at least one component at market rate: either 7% gross royalty with 0.5% equity, or 3% gross with 2.5% equity.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="'Phantom Equity' Instead of Real Ownership" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Company offers "phantom shares" or "profit interests" instead of actual equity. Sounds the same — but phantom equity has no voting rights, no board access, no legal ownership protections, can be canceled at board discretion, and has worse tax treatment.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Phantom equity is a promise, not ownership. It can be modified or canceled without your consent. Always demand actual common stock, stock options (ISOs if possible), or restricted stock units — instruments with legal protections.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>I need actual ownership — common stock or ISOs — not phantom equity or profit interests. Phantom shares lack voting rights, legal protections, and favorable tax treatment. If the company can't issue real equity, I need significantly higher royalties to compensate: 10–12% gross instead of 7%.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Equity Dilution Without Protection" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** You negotiate 3% equity. Series A: diluted to 2.1%. Series B: 1.4%. Series C: 0.9%. By exit: 0.4%. Your "3% equity" is worth 87% less than what you negotiated. Company raises capital, your percentage evaporates, and no one tells you until exit.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Dilution is inevitable but protections are negotiable. Weighted average anti-dilution adjusts your conversion price downward when shares are issued below your price. Pro-rata rights let you invest to maintain your percentage.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>I need weighted average anti-dilution protection and pro-rata participation rights. Without protections, my 3% becomes 0.4% by exit. Anti-dilution adjusts my conversion if shares are issued below my price, and pro-rata lets me invest to maintain percentage. These are standard protections.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

<SbSection id="negotiate">

<SbHeading>Negotiation</SbHeading>

Hybrid negotiation centers on the royalty/equity ratio — trade between components based on risk tolerance and income needs.

<SbNeg tabsJson='["High Leverage", "Moderate", "Low Leverage"]'>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Premium on both components.** "My contribution drives both product sales and company valuation. I need 10% gross wholesale royalty + 3% equity with full anti-dilution and acceleration. This reflects my track record and the commercial impact I'll deliver."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Escalating royalty at milestones** (8% to $5M, 10% to $15M, 12% above). Pro-rata rights on equity. Board observer seat. Quarterly royalty reporting with annual audit rights. Single-trigger 100% acceleration.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Essential contributor, multiple partners competing.** Your brand/name drives sales. Proven commercial track record. Can walk away. Partner needs you more than you need them.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Balanced hybrid with component flexibility.** "I can weight toward royalty or equity depending on company stage. Need-cash version: 8% royalty + 1.5% equity. Betting-on-growth version: 5% royalty + 3% equity. Which aligns with your capital needs?"</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Escalators bridging the gap.** "Start at 6% royalty + 1% equity. If revenue exceeds $5M Year 1, royalty increases to 8% and equity increases to 2%. Performance triggers protect both of us."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Valuable contributor with growing track record.** Both parties believe in the product. You want alignment and can flex between royalty and equity emphasis based on mutual needs.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Income-weighted hybrid with modest equity.** "I need 6% gross royalty to make this work financially. I'll accept 0.75–1% equity as upside. The royalty is non-negotiable — it's how I pay my rent. Equity is how we align on long-term success."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Protect the royalty component above all else.** Gross definition, quarterly reporting, audit rights. Equity protections important but secondary — if equity fails, royalties must sustain you.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**First hybrid deal, need income security.** Product has potential but unproven. Better to learn hybrid mechanics with strong royalty floor than gamble on equity-heavy structure you don't fully understand.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
</SbNeg>

<SbSection id="value">

<SbHeading>Example Value Calculation: Maya's Kitchen Product Partnership</SbHeading>

Maya is a product designer partnering with a kitchen brand to create a signature cookware line. She negotiates a royalty + equity hybrid.

<SbChart title="7-Year Outcome — Hybrid vs. Royalty-Only vs. Equity-Only">
  <SbChartRow label="Hybrid (7% royalty + 2% equity)" value="$5M" pct="100" />
  <SbChartRow label="Royalty only (10%)" value="$2.86M" pct="57" />
  <SbChartRow label="Equity only (5%)" value="$3.75M" pct="75" />
</SbChart>

**The deal:** 7% gross wholesale royalty (escalating: 7% to $5M, 9% to $15M, 11% above) + 2% equity (4-year vest, 1-year cliff, single-trigger acceleration). Company valued at $15M.

**Royalty component (7 years):**

- Years 1–3: $100K → $250K → $400K = $750K
- Years 4–7: $400K → $350K → $300K → $200K = $1.25M
- **Total royalties: $2M**

**Equity component:**

- 2% diluted to 1.2% through 2 funding rounds (anti-dilution limited damage)
- Company acquired Year 7 for $250M
- Maya's 1.2%: **$3M**

**Combined: $2M royalties + $3M equity = $5M.** Royalty-only (10% gross) would have yielded $2.86M. Equity-only (5%) would have yielded $3.75M. **The hybrid outperformed both** because it captured value from both product sales AND company appreciation.

<SbHeading>Example Use Cases</SbHeading>

How three creatives structured royalty + equity hybrids — and what the dual-component design meant for outcomes.

<SbCase label="Case 01 · Consumer Products · Mid-Career" name="Maya Rodriguez — Kitchen Brand Partnership" tag="Product Design / Kitchen" outcome="$5M over 7 years (hybrid outperformed both single components)">
  <SbCaseText>Maya, 32, an industrial designer with 200K Instagram followers, was approached by a kitchen startup ($2M revenue, growing 80% YoY). Initial offer: 5% net royalty + 1.5% equity. Maya's attorney benchmarked both components and countered: 7% gross escalating + 2% equity with anti-dilution.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** Royalties: $2M over 7 years (cookware line sold through Target and direct). Equity: company acquired Year 7 for $250M, Maya's diluted 1.2% = $3M. **Total: $5M.** If she'd taken royalty-only (10%): $2.86M. If equity-only (5%): $3.75M (but volatile, dependent on single exit event). Hybrid delivered the most total value AND the most stable income path.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** "Royalties paid my bills and let me quit my agency job Year 2. Equity felt like a lottery ticket — until it wasn't. The hybrid meant I never had to choose between paying rent and building wealth. When the acquisition happened, it was a $3M bonus on top of $2M I'd already earned. Neither component alone would have matched the combined outcome."</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 02 · Music · Established" name="Marcus Williams — Streaming Platform Deal" tag="Music / R&B" outcome="$3M over 5 years (equity doubled total earnings)">
  <SbCaseText>Marcus, 28, an independent R&B artist with 50M annual Spotify streams, chose between two offers: Established platform ($0.0045/stream, no equity, $200K advance) vs. Growth-stage platform ($0.0035/stream + 0.5% equity, $100K advance). Expected value modeling: established = $1.54M over 5 years. Growth-stage = $1.18M streaming + $0.7–6M equity (scenario-dependent).</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** Marcus chose the growth-stage platform. Streaming royalties: $1.4M over 5 years. Platform acquired Year 5 for $400M. His 0.4% (diluted from 0.5%): $1.6M. **Total: $3M** vs. $1.54M from the established platform (1.95x more). The equity component doubled his total earnings — transforming a "worse streaming deal" into a career-changing outcome.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** "Everyone told me to take the higher per-stream rate. But I modeled the expected value: even with 70% chance the platform failed, the equity's expected value exceeded the streaming difference. I deliberately chose lower per-stream for equity exposure. The platform's success was lucky — but the structure was strategic."</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 03 · Fashion · Emerging (Cautionary)" name="Sofia Chen — Both Components Below Market" tag="Fashion / Accessories" outcome="$83K over 3 years (below single-component alternative)">
  <SbCaseText>Sofia, 26, a jewelry designer, accepted a "hybrid deal" with a DTC accessories brand: 3% net royalty + 0.5% equity. She was excited about the hybrid label. Her attorney later calculated: 3% net = ~1.2% gross (market: 6–10%). 0.5% equity diluted to 0.2% (market: 2–3%). Both components were 75% below market.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The result:** 3 years: royalties $68K ($23K/year — below market by $40K+ annually). Equity: company valued at $8M, her 0.2% = $16K (not worth exercising after tax). **Total: $83K.** If she'd negotiated 7% gross royalty alone: ~$200K over same period. The "hybrid" earned 58% less than a single good component would have.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** "I was seduced by 'hybrid' — it sounded sophisticated. But bad royalty + bad equity is worse than good royalty alone. My attorney — hired too late — showed me: 7% gross royalty would have tripled my income. Now I benchmark each component separately against industry standards. If either is below market, the 'hybrid' label doesn't make it a good deal."</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>
