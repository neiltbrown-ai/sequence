<SbSection id="overview">

<SbHeading>Overview</SbHeading>

The Retainer + Bonus Model solves the fundamental cash flow problem that plagues most creative professionals: income volatility. One month you invoice $30K across three projects, the next you earn $3K while chasing new business. This feast-or-famine cycle creates financial stress, forces short-term thinking, and prevents investment in long-term career development. Retainers transform this chaos into predictable monthly income.

A retainer is a monthly fee — typically **$5K–$50K+** — paid for ongoing access to your expertise, regardless of specific deliverables. Three $10K/month retainer clients generate **$360K annually** in predictable income. But pure retainers have a ceiling problem: your income is capped regardless of value created. If your work helps a client raise $50M, you still receive the same $10K/month. Performance bonuses solve this — they add uncapped upside to your stable base.

The optimal structure combines meaningful monthly retainer (**60–80% of your total compensation target**) with bonuses tied to objective metrics — revenue growth, fundraising, product launches, user acquisition. Your downside is protected by guaranteed income. Your upside is unlimited through bonuses that scale with client success. **Asymmetric risk-reward: capped downside, unlimited upside.** This is the foundation model for sustainable creative careers.

<SbSubheading>Bonus Structures</SbSubheading>

Performance bonuses come in four primary forms. The key principle: **metrics must be objective and verifiable.** "We'll pay if we're happy" never pays.

<SbTabs tabsJson='["Revenue-Based", "Fundraising", "Product Launch", "Acquisition"]'>
  <SbTabPanel>
    <SbTabGrid>
      <SbTabItem label="Structure" value="Tiered milestones" />
      <SbTabItem label="Typical Bonus" value="$15K–$60K per tier" />
      <SbTabItem label="Alternative" value="2% of growth above baseline" />
    </SbTabGrid>
    <SbTabNote>**Example:** $12K/month retainer. $15K bonus at $3M ARR, $30K at $10M ARR, $60K at $25M ARR. Or: 2% of growth above $5M baseline — company grows to $8M, your bonus is $60K.</SbTabNote>
  </SbTabPanel>
  <SbTabPanel>
    <SbTabGrid>
      <SbTabItem label="Structure" value="Flat fee or % of capital" />
      <SbTabItem label="Flat Fee" value="$50–100K per round" />
      <SbTabItem label="Percentage" value="1% of capital raised" />
    </SbTabGrid>
    <SbTabNote>**Example:** $15K/month retainer. $50K when Series A closes, $100K when Series B closes. Or: 1% of capital raised — $10M Series A = $100K bonus. Often capped at $250K per round.</SbTabNote>
  </SbTabPanel>
  <SbTabPanel>
    <SbTabGrid>
      <SbTabItem label="Structure" value="Launch + adoption milestones" />
      <SbTabItem label="Launch Bonus" value="$25K on-time ship" />
      <SbTabItem label="Adoption Bonus" value="$40K at 10K users" />
    </SbTabGrid>
    <SbTabNote>**Caution:** Be careful with "on time" clauses — delays outside your control can cost you. Use "within 30 days of target" language instead of exact dates.</SbTabNote>
  </SbTabPanel>
  <SbTabPanel>
    <SbTabGrid>
      <SbTabItem label="Structure" value="User count milestones" />
      <SbTabItem label="Typical Tiers" value="$10K / $25K / $60K" />
      <SbTabItem label="At Milestones" value="50K / 250K / 1M users" />
    </SbTabGrid>
    <SbTabNote>**Alternative:** CAC improvement bonus — $30K if Customer Acquisition Cost reduced by 30%+ over 12 months. Ties your bonus to efficiency, not just growth.</SbTabNote>

<SbFlags>
  <SbFlagCol header="Use When">

    <SbFlag type="use">Relationship is genuinely long-term (12+ months expected)</SbFlag>

    <SbFlag type="avoid">Bonus metrics are subjective ("we'll pay if we're happy" never pays)</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="avoid">Client is financially unstable — burning cash with short runway</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="avoid">The work is truly one-time — don't force a retainer on a project</SbFlag>

<SbSection id="protect">

<SbHeading>Key Protections</SbHeading>

Four contract provisions that separate sustainable retainers from exploitative ones. Every retainer agreement should include all four.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="Scope Definition Clarity">
    <SbCardText>Essential contract language: "Retainer includes [LIST SPECIFIC DELIVERABLES OR HOURS]. Out of scope work billed at $X/hour or quoted separately." Example: 2 strategy sessions/month, unlimited async Slack, 1 quarterly strategy document.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Without this, "unlimited access" becomes "unlimited work for fixed fee" — and your effective rate collapses.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Bonus Metric Objectivity">
    <SbCardText>**Good language:** "Bonus of $50K payable when Company achieves $10M ARR as reported in audited financials, verified by third-party audit if requested." **Bad language:** "Bonus payable if Company is satisfied with performance."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Objective metrics can't be disputed. Subjective "satisfaction" clauses are designed to avoid payment.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Termination Protection">
    <SbCardText>60-day written notice from either party. Client pays retainer for full notice period regardless of work performed. Critical clause: "Bonuses earned for milestones achieved during retainer term OR within 90 days of termination remain payable."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Prevents strategic cancellation right before a bonus triggers — one of the most common retainer manipulations.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Annual Rate Escalation">
    <SbCardText>"Retainer increases by 10% annually on anniversary date, or by mutual agreement to market rate, whichever is greater." Client may opt out by providing 90 days notice of termination.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Prevents rate stagnation. Your retainer should grow as your value compounds — without this clause, you're giving a discount that deepens every year.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

<SbSection id="manipulations">

<SbHeading>Common Manipulations</SbHeading>

Four tactics that erode retainer value. Knowing them before you're at the table changes the negotiation entirely.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="The 'Unlimited Hours' Expectation" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Client pays $8K/month retainer, expects 40+ hours of work weekly. Math: $8K ÷ 160 hours = $50/hour effective rate. If your market rate is $200/hour, you're working for 25% of your value.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** This isn't a retainer — it's discounted full-time employment without benefits, PTO, or equity. The "retainer" label disguises the discount.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>The retainer is for strategic advisory access, not full-time equivalent work. If you need 40 hours weekly, that's a different engagement at $30–40K/month minimum. This retainer covers 12 hours of high-leverage strategic guidance.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Scope Creep via 'Quick Favor'" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "Hey, can you quickly review this deck? Just 5 slides." Turns into 3 hours of rework, another meeting, and a follow-up. Repeated monthly, these "quick favors" double your actual hours without adjusting compensation.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Death by a thousand cuts. Each request seems small, but they accumulate into major unpaid work that erodes your effective rate.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Happy to help with that — should we bill it as project work or adjust our retainer scope? This month included 8 hours of work outside our agreement, valued at $4,800. Let's either adjust the retainer or bill these separately going forward.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Bonus Metric Moving Target" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Original agreement: "$50K bonus when company hits $10M ARR." Company reaches $9.8M — client says "let's see if we sustain it next quarter." Hits $10.2M — client disputes the measurement period.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Vague metric definitions are designed to create wiggle room. The goalposts move because the contract lets them.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Per our agreement, the bonus of $50K is payable within 30 days of Company achieving $10M Annual Recurring Revenue, calculated as trailing 12-month revenue from date of achievement. I'm showing that milestone was reached on [date]. Congratulations — I'll send the invoice this week.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Cancellation Before Bonus Triggers" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Retainer running 10 months. Company about to close Series B. Client terminates retainer 2 weeks before close with 30-day notice. Bonus triggers 15 days later — but you're no longer retained.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Strategic timing of cancellation to avoid bonus payment. Without a tail clause, you have no recourse.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Our agreement includes a 90-day tail clause — bonuses earned for milestones achieved during the retainer term or within 90 days of termination remain payable regardless of termination date. The Series B close falls within that window.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

<SbSection id="negotiate">

<SbHeading>Negotiation</SbHeading>

Your leverage determines your strategy. Three approaches based on how much the counterparty needs your ongoing strategic guidance versus general execution support.

<SbNeg tabsJson='["High Leverage", "Moderate", "Low Leverage"]'>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Quote retainer at 70% of equivalent project value** with bonuses for the remaining 30%. "Four campaigns annually = $300K project-based. A $20K/month retainer with performance bonuses gives you priority access at better value."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Include performance bonuses tied to 2–3 objective metrics** the client already tracks — revenue, conversion, fundraising. Push for annual rate escalation clause.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Client approached you,** has urgent strategic need, referral from trusted source, or you're one of few with the required expertise.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Accept lower base if bonus structure improves.** "I can do $X/month with the same bonus structure. This reflects ~60% of project-based value with 40% in performance upside."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Lock in non-negotiables** — scope definition, objective bonus metrics, 60-day termination notice, annual rate increase. These matter more than the base number.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Client is comparing options** but has engaged seriously with your process. Multiple qualified alternatives exist.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Offer a project-based proof of concept** before committing to retainer. "Let's start with a defined project. If it goes well, we can discuss an ongoing structure."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Propose a 3-month trial retainer** with clear success criteria for renewal. Reduces commitment anxiety for both parties.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**You need the client more than they need you.** Don't discount the monthly rate — restructure the engagement instead.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
</SbNeg>

<SbSection id="value">

<SbHeading>Example Value Calculation: Kai's Retainer Portfolio</SbHeading>

Kai is a fractional creative director with 12 years of experience. Instead of chasing individual projects at $300/hour, he builds a portfolio of four retainer clients over 18 months.

**Client A** — Tech startup: $8K/month, 12 hours. Bonus: $15K when Series B closes.
**Client B** — Consumer brand: $10K/month, 15 hours. Bonus: $20K if revenue grows 30%+ YoY.
**Client C** — Nonprofit: $5K/month, 8 hours. No bonus (mission-driven).
**Client D** — E-commerce: $7K/month, 10 hours. Bonus: $10K if email revenue grows 50%+.

<SbChart title="Kai's Annual Income — Retainer Portfolio vs. Equivalent Hourly">
  <SbChartRow label="Retainer portfolio + bonuses" value="$385K" pct="100" />
  <SbChartRow label="Equivalent hourly (same hours)" value="$162K" pct="42" />
</SbChart>

**Without the structure:** Kai bills 45 hours/month at $300/hour = $162K annually. He's constantly pitching, writing proposals, and chasing invoices. Losing one client means scrambling for the next.

**With the structure:** Base retainers total $360K. Bonuses add $25K (Client A's Series B closed, Client D's email revenue grew 65%). Total: **$385K** working the same 45 hours/month. Effective rate: **$713/hour.** If he loses one client, 67–75% of his income stays intact.

**What changed:** Kai stopped selling hours and started selling ongoing strategic access. The portfolio approach means no single client can break him — and the compounding relationships generate referrals that fill any gaps.

<SbHeading>Example Use Cases</SbHeading>

How three creatives at different career stages structured retainer deals — and what happened.

<SbCase label="Case 01 · SaaS · Mid-Career" name="Nadia Osei — Fractional CMO" tag="SaaS / Tech" outcome="$364K over 2 years">
  <SbCaseText>Nadia was offered $8K/month for "marketing leadership, 15–20 hours per week" at a Series A B2B SaaS company. The math: 60–80 hours/month at $8K = $100–133/hour effective — a 50% discount from her $250 project rate. She recognized the structure was exploitative disguised as opportunity.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** She restructured to **$10K/month for 12 hours** (strategic advisory, not execution), with $20K bonus at $5M ARR and $50K when Series B closes. Year 1: $120K retainer + $70K in bonuses = $190K. Year 2: rate increased to $12K/month per agreement + $30K bonus = $174K. Two-year total: **$364K** — 1.9x the original offer.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** Redefining scope from 60 hours of execution to 12 hours of strategy was the decisive move. Same client, same relationship — 5x the effective rate. The bonuses rewarded outcomes she was already driving.</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 02 · E-commerce · Established" name="Tomás Reyes — Creative Director" tag="E-commerce / DTC" outcome="$704K over 2 years">
  <SbCaseText>A $20M DTC brand approached Tomás about ongoing creative direction for 4 major campaigns per year. Project-based, the work was worth ~$400K annually. He didn't need the retainer — he already had two at $15K/month each. But the relationship was strong.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** He proposed **$20K/month** with $30K bonuses per campaign achieving ≥4x ROAS and $50K if brand revenue grew 40%+ YoY. Year 1: $240K retainer + $60K bonuses (2 of 4 campaigns hit) = $300K. Year 2 (10% increase): $264K retainer + $140K bonuses (3 campaigns + revenue target) = **$404K.**</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** Not every bonus hits — and that's fine. 5 of 9 possible bonuses achieved across 2 years is realistic. The base retainer covered needs; bonuses were genuine upside. Quality of life improvement (stable relationship vs. constant pitching) was worth the slight discount from project-based equivalents.</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 03 · CPG · Emerging" name="Maya Lindström — Brand Strategist" tag="CPG / Startup" outcome="$104K + equity in 12 months">
  <SbCaseText>Maya, 5 years in, was offered $6K/month for "everything marketing and brand" plus 0.5% equity by a pre-revenue food startup with $2M seed funding. She recognized two red flags: undefined scope and dependence on equity that might never vest.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** She restructured into phases. **Phase 1 (pre-launch):** $8K/month with $15K launch bonus. **Phase 2 (post-launch):** $6K/month with $20K at $1M revenue and $30K at Series A. She reduced equity to 0.3% — treating cash as real compensation and equity as bonus. Total cash: **$104K** over 12 months (38% more than original offer). The company later cancelled at month 13 when runway shrunk.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** Startup retainers are inherently risky — be prepared for cancellation. Phase-based structures match rate to intensity. Maya didn't depend on equity as real compensation, and her launch timing bonus clause ("on time") cost her $15K when the product shipped 2 weeks late. Lesson: use "within 30 days" language, not exact dates.</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>
