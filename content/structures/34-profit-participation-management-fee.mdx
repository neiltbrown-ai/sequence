<SbSection id="overview">

<SbHeading>Overview</SbHeading>

Profit Participation + Management Fee is a dual compensation structure combining **(1) regular management fee** — guaranteed monthly or quarterly payment for ongoing oversight and advisory — with **(2) profit participation** — a percentage of profits generated above a threshold. Instead of choosing between guaranteed income (fee-only) or performance upside (participation-only), you negotiate both. Management fee compensates you for time, expertise, and opportunity cost. Profit participation rewards actual value creation beyond baseline expectations.

The principle: management work has value independent of outcomes, but extraordinary results deserve extraordinary compensation. Fund managers receive 2% AUM plus 20% carried interest (the "2 and 20" standard). Creative directors receive $200K–$500K annual fee plus 15% of net profits. Brand ambassadors receive $10K–$50K monthly plus 10–25% of product profits. The structure works wherever your ongoing strategic oversight drives entity profitability.

Combined, these create asymmetric advantage: if the entity thrives, both components pay. If profits are modest, the fee sustains you. If you leave, trailing participation captures value from work you set in motion. Over a 3–5 year engagement, this structure typically delivers **2–5x more than fee-only** in successful scenarios while providing an acceptable floor if profits disappoint.

<SbHeading>When to Use This Structure</SbHeading>

<SbFlags>
  <SbFlagCol header="Use When">
    <SbFlag type="use">Providing ongoing strategic oversight (not one-time execution)</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="use">Entity has existing revenue and profit potential (not pre-revenue)</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="use">Your involvement meaningfully impacts profitability</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="use">Entity is transparent with financials (willing to share P&L, accept audits)</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="use">Management fee alone covers your minimum income needs</SbFlag>
  </SbFlagCol>
  <SbFlagCol header="Avoid When">
    <SbFlag type="avoid">Entity is pre-revenue with no path to profitability (use equity instead)</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="avoid">Entity refuses financial transparency or audit rights</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="avoid">Management fee is token (&lt;25% of market rate) relying on participation</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="avoid">Profit participation is "net" with unlimited deductions (see #23)</SbFlag>
    <SbFlag type="avoid">Relationship is short-term or project-based (use milestones #33)</SbFlag>
  </SbFlagCol>
</SbFlags>

<SbSection id="protect">

<SbHeading>Key Protections</SbHeading>

Five provisions that ensure both the fee and participation components deliver real value.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="Gross Profit or EBITDA Participation (Never Net)">
    <SbCardText>"Participation calculated on EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization) OR Gross Profit (revenue minus direct COGS only)." Net profit participation with unlimited deductions routinely shows $0 on profitable entities. On $10M revenue: EBITDA participation at 20% = $400K. Net profit participation at 20% after inflated overhead = $40K. Same entity, 10x difference.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** If forced into net profit, cap all deductions: overhead maximum 15% of revenue, marketing maximum 25%, total deductions cannot exceed 60% of gross. Participation floor: never less than 20% of gross profit regardless of accounting treatments.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Management Fee as Senior Obligation (Payment Priority)">
    <SbCardText>"Management Fee is senior obligation payable before: (a) discretionary bonuses, (b) distributions to equity holders, (c) non-essential capital expenditures, (d) related-party fees. If delayed >15 days past due, Manager may suspend services. If delayed >30 days, Manager may terminate retaining all fees paid plus pro-rata current period."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Without priority, cash-strapped entities pay everyone else first and delay your fee. Priority ensures you're paid alongside (or before) other obligations. Suspension rights create strong incentive for timely payment.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Non-Recoupable Fee (Not an Advance Against Participation)">
    <SbCardText>"Management Fee is separate, guaranteed compensation for ongoing work — NOT an advance or draw against profit participation. Fee and participation are independent streams. Fee is earned by providing services. Participation is earned by generating profits. Neither reduces or offsets the other."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** "Draw against participation" means your fee is a loan — if participation is insufficient, you owe the difference. This shifts all risk to you and eliminates income security. Fee and participation must be legally separate compensation streams.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Quarterly Reporting + Audit Rights with Fee Recovery">
    <SbCardText>"Entity provides quarterly P&L within 30 days of quarter-end showing revenue, costs by category, and participation calculation. Manager may audit annually with 30 days notice. If underpayment >5%, Entity pays underpayment + 1.5% monthly interest + audit costs up to $25K."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Without reporting, you trust self-reported numbers from the entity benefiting from lower profit. Without audit rights, there's no verification mechanism. Fee recovery creates deterrent: honest reporting costs nothing; dishonest reporting costs the audit plus interest.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Trailing Participation Post-Termination">
    <SbCardText>"Upon termination: 100% participation on profits through termination date. 50% participation for 12 months post-termination. 25% participation for months 13–24. Reflects value created by Manager that continues generating profits after relationship ends."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Without trailing, entities terminate right before large profit periods — timing termination to avoid paying participation on value you created. Trailing ensures you capture value from initiatives that take 12–24 months to generate returns.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

</SbSection>

<SbSection id="manipulations">

<SbHeading>Common Manipulations</SbHeading>

Four tactics that exploit the dual-component structure to underpay on both.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="Token Fee + Unrealistic Profit Projections" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "We'll pay $3K/month ($36K annually) plus 25% of profits! We're projecting $5M profit next year — that's $1.25M for you!" The $36K fee is 75% below market. If profits don't materialize (they rarely match projections), you've worked for $36K. If they do, the participation might be calculated on "net" profit that never appears.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Management fee must stand alone as acceptable compensation. Participation is bonus upside, not core income. If the entity can't afford market-rate fee, they can't afford a senior advisor.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>$36K doesn't cover my overhead for this commitment level. Market rate for comparable advisory is $150–250K annually. I need minimum $150K management fee. I'm happy with 25% participation as upside, but the fee must work as standalone compensation. If profits materialize, participation is bonus. If they don't, I've still been fairly paid for my work.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="'Net Profit' with Unlimited Cost Allocations" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "20% of net profits — revenue less all costs including but not limited to COGS, marketing, overhead, administrative, and any other expenses." On $10M revenue: $4M COGS + $2M marketing + $3M overhead + $800K "other" = $200K net. Your 20%: $40K. On EBITDA: $10M – $4M = $6M × 20% = $1.2M. Same entity, 30x difference.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** "Including but not limited to" means unlimited expense categories. Demand EBITDA or gross profit. If forced into net, cap every deduction explicitly.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Net profit with unlimited deductions rarely pays out fairly. I need participation on EBITDA or gross profit. Using your $10M revenue: EBITDA after $4M COGS = $6M, my 20% = $1.2M. If net profit is necessary, I need explicit caps: overhead max 15%, marketing max 25%, total deductions cannot exceed 55% of revenue. This creates a floor of ~9% of revenue as my minimum participation.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Management Fee as 'Draw Against Participation'" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "$15K monthly draw against future participation. Year-end: if participation > $180K, you get the difference. If participation < $180K, deficit carries forward." Year 1: $180K draw, $150K participation = –$30K deficit. Year 2: $180K draw, $200K = $20K net (after deficit). You've worked 2 years for $200K total.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Draw-against-participation is exploitative — converts guaranteed income into a loan. Fee and participation must be separate, non-recoupable streams.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Management fee must be separate compensation, not an advance against participation. These are two different value streams: the fee compensates my time and expertise, participation rewards value creation. I won't accept a structure where my fee becomes a loan I repay from uncertain future profits. Separate, non-recoupable streams — or full fee with no participation.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Termination Forfeits Earned Participation" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "Upon termination, Manager's right to future participation ceases immediately. Participation owed but not yet paid is forfeited." You work January–December, participation paid annually in February. Entity terminates December 15. You forfeit 12 months of earned participation.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Participation earned through service should survive termination. The entity timed termination to avoid paying for your full year of value creation.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Earned participation must survive termination. I'm entitled to participation on profits generated during periods I actively provided services, regardless of payment date. Plus trailing: 50% for 12 months post-termination, 25% for months 13–24. My work creates value that takes 12–24 months to fully realize — trailing participation captures that.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

<SbSection id="negotiate">

<SbHeading>Negotiation</SbHeading>

Fee + participation negotiation centers on establishing the fee at market rate FIRST, then layering participation as upside.

<SbNeg tabsJson='["High Leverage", "Moderate", "Low Leverage"]'>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Market-rate fee + tiered escalating participation.** "Management fee of $300K annually (market rate for this role). Plus tiered EBITDA participation: 15% on first $2M, 20% on $2–5M, 25% above $5M. As I drive profitability beyond baseline, my participation increases."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Trailing participation 50%/25% for 24 months post-termination.** Change-of-control acceleration (3-year trailing average as lump sum). Board observer seat. Quarterly reporting with annual audit. Fee payment priority.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Proven profit driver, multiple entities competing.** Track record of increasing margins 5–15% annually. Can demonstrate specific ROI from previous engagements. Can walk away.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Escalating fee + standard participation.** "Year 1: $175K fee + 12% EBITDA. Year 2: $200K + 12% (if revenue exceeds $5M). Year 3: $225K + 15% (if revenue exceeds $7M). Escalators reward our joint success."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**If entity resists market-rate fee, trade for higher participation:** "I can accept $150K fee if participation increases to 18%. Lower guaranteed income offset by larger upside." Always benchmark each component independently.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Growing track record, first engagement at this level.** Entity values your expertise but has budget constraints. Escalating structure gives entity breathing room while rewarding growth.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Acceptable fee + modest participation with core protections.** "I need $135K management fee minimum — that covers my overhead. I'll accept 10% EBITDA participation. The fee is non-negotiable; participation percentage is flexible. But EBITDA basis and quarterly reporting are essential."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Protect the fee above all else.** At low leverage, participation may never materialize — the fee IS your compensation. Never accept fee below 75% of market rate regardless of participation promises.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**First fee + participation deal.** Learning the mechanics. Better to establish strong fee floor than gamble on participation you can't yet predict.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
</SbNeg>

<SbSection id="value">

<SbHeading>Example Value Calculation: Rachel's Creative Studio Directorship</SbHeading>

Rachel, a creative director with 15 years agency experience, takes a boutique studio CD role. She compares three compensation structures over 3 years.

<SbChart title="3-Year Outcome — Fee + Participation vs. Alternatives">
  <SbChartRow label="Fee + participation ($135K→$175K + 12→15% EBITDA)" value="$915K" pct="100" />
  <SbChartRow label="Full-time CD salary + bonus" value="$810K" pct="89" />
  <SbChartRow label="Fee-only (no participation)" value="$525K" pct="57" />
</SbChart>

**Fee-only (freelance CD rate):** $175K annually × 3 years = **$525K.**

**Full-time salaried CD:** $230K salary + $40K bonus = $270K × 3 years = **$810K** (includes benefits).

**Fee + participation (escalating):**

- Year 1: $135K fee + 12% × $900K EBITDA = $135K + $108K = **$243K**
- Year 2: $155K fee + 12% × $1.1M EBITDA = $155K + $132K = **$287K**
- Year 3: $175K fee + 15% × $1.4M EBITDA = $175K + $210K = **$385K**
- **3-year total: $915K** ($305K annual average)

**What made the difference:** Rachel's strategic initiatives grew studio revenue from $4M to $6.5M over 3 years, increasing EBITDA from $900K to $1.4M. Her participation captured 12–15% of that growth — rewarding value creation that a flat salary or fee would have ignored. The escalating fee structure gave the studio breathing room in Year 1 while ensuring Rachel's base grew with the business.

<SbHeading>Example Use Cases</SbHeading>

How three creatives structured management fee + participation — and what the dual-component design meant for outcomes.

<SbCase label="Case 01 · Creative Studio · Mid-Career" name="Rachel Kim — Boutique Studio Creative Director" tag="Design / Agency" outcome="$915K over 3 years (1.75x fee-only, 1.13x salaried)">
  <SbCaseText>Rachel, 38, was offered CD at a boutique studio ($4M revenue, 12-person team). Initial offer: $6K/month ($72K) + 30% profits. Her mentor flagged it: $72K is junior designer pay, and "30% profits" likely means net profit that never appears. She benchmarked market rate ($150–250K for boutique CD) and countered.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** Final deal — Year 1: $135K + 12% EBITDA. Year 2: $155K + 12% (triggered at $4.5M revenue). Year 3: $175K + 15% (triggered at $6M revenue). Over 3 years: $915K total. Initial offer would have yielded $72K × 3 = $216K fee + uncertain "net profit" participation (likely $0–$50K annually after overhead manipulation). **Rachel's counter-negotiation generated $550K+ additional value.**</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** "The original offer sounded generous — 30% profits! But 30% of manipulated net is worthless. My attorney showed me: 12% of EBITDA is worth more than 30% of net because EBITDA can't be manipulated the same way. And the escalating fee meant my guaranteed income grew as the studio grew. If EBITDA had been $300K, I still earned $135K base — below market but livable. The participation was genuine bonus, not my survival income."</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 02 · Fund Management · Established" name="David Chen — First Fund '1.5 and 25'" tag="Venture Capital / Fund" outcome="$13M over 10 years (fee sustained operations, carry created wealth)">
  <SbCaseText>David, 42, launched a $25M venture fund. Industry standard: 2% AUM + 20% carry. His anchor LP pushed back on 2% for a first-time manager. David countered: "1.5% fee + 25% carry. You save on fees, I earn more carry — but only if I deliver returns." LPs accepted: lower annual cost, higher alignment.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** Management fees: $375K annually × 10 years = $3.75M (after reducing to 1% in harvest period). Portfolio returned 4.6x: $115M on $25M. Carried interest: 25% × $36M (profits above 8% hurdle) = $9M. Own capital (1%): $900K gain. **Total: ~$13M over 10 years.** At standard "2 and 20": $10M fees + $7.2M carry = $17.2M — but LPs wouldn't have accepted 2% from a first-time manager. The 1.5/25 structure got the fund raised.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** "Management fees kept me alive for 10 years. Carry didn't pay until Years 6–10 — if I'd taken 0% fee and 30% carry, I'd have starved before the exits happened. The fee wasn't luxurious ($275K after fund expenses), but it let me focus on building the portfolio. And 25% vs. 20% carry netted me $1.8M extra — the LPs gladly traded $1.25M in fee savings over the decade for that."</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 03 · Brand · Emerging (Cautionary)" name="Sofia Reyes — Token Fee Trap" tag="Consumer Brand / Advisory" outcome="$147K over 2 years (below market on both components)">
  <SbCaseText>Sofia, 30, was offered brand advisory for a DTC skincare company: $4K/month ($48K annually) + 20% of "profits." She was excited about the participation percentage. Two years later: $48K × 2 = $96K in fees (57% below $110K market rate). Profit participation: 20% of "net profit" after founder salary ($200K), overhead allocation ($150K), and marketing ($300K) left $50K in "profit." Her 20%: $10K/year × 2 = $20K. **Total: $116K.** On EBITDA ($500K): 20% would have been $100K/year.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** "I was seduced by '20% of profits.' But 20% of net profit after the founder pays herself $200K and allocates $150K in overhead is 20% of almost nothing. If I'd negotiated $110K fee alone — no participation — I'd have earned $220K over 2 years instead of $116K. The 'hybrid' structure was worse than a simple fee because both components were below market. Now I benchmark each component separately before agreeing to any dual structure."</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>
