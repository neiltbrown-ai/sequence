<SbSection id="overview">

<SbHeading>Overview</SbHeading>

The Advisory/Consultant Model represents a fundamental shift from selling time and execution to selling judgment. Rather than competing on deliverables — designs, code, content — advisors compete on insight, helping clients make better decisions, avoid expensive mistakes, and identify opportunities others miss. A creative advisor who has navigated 50 product launches can help a client avoid $500K in mistakes in a 10-hour strategic engagement. The value isn't in what you produce — it's in what you prevent and what you illuminate.

The economic logic is compelling: execution work scales linearly (more hours = more revenue), but advisory work captures value asymmetrically. Ten hours of strategic guidance might save a client $1M or unlock $5M in opportunity. Premium advisors command **$500–2,000/hour** or **$25–100K/month retainers** because their judgment creates outsized value relative to time invested. Four to six advisory clients at $40K/month generates **$1.9–2.9M annually** working 60–96 hours/month — versus $400K working 130+ hours in execution.

Strategically, the Advisory Model is the natural evolution of premium service positioning (#1) and retainer work (#2). Once you've demonstrated execution excellence and built a reputation, transitioning to advisory increases your effective rate, reduces time-intensive work, and positions you for equity deals, board seats, and long-term partnership structures. **It's where experience stops being traded for money and starts compounding as an asset.**

<SbSection id="protect">

<SbHeading>Key Protections</SbHeading>

Advisory work requires different boundaries than execution. Four provisions that prevent your strategic value from being extracted without compensation.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="Scope Separation (Advisory ≠ Execution)">
    <SbCardText>"Engagement includes strategic advisory sessions, frameworks, and recommendations. Execution, implementation, and production work are outside scope and priced separately." Be explicit: you provide direction, they (or their team, or your referral partners) execute.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** The number one advisory failure mode is scope drift from strategy to execution. Once you're implementing your own recommendations at advisory rates, you've lost leverage — and the client will expect execution at those rates permanently.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="C-Suite Access Requirement">
    <SbCardText>"Advisory services require direct access to [CEO/Founder/decision-maker]. Strategic recommendations are most effective when discussed directly with final decision authority." Don't work through middle managers — advisory only works when your guidance reaches people who can act on it.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** If your recommendations pass through 3 layers before reaching a decision-maker, they'll be diluted, delayed, or ignored. You lose effectiveness — then the client says advisory "didn't work."</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Minimum Engagement Term">
    <SbCardText>6-month minimum commitment for retainer advisory, with 60-day termination notice. Strategic advisory requires time to implement recommendations and measure results — month-to-month arrangements never demonstrate real impact.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Clients who want "just one month to test" extract strategic insights then disengage before paying for ongoing value. Minimum terms ensure the relationship has time to prove itself.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="IP Retention (Your Frameworks Stay Yours)">
    <SbCardText>"Advisor retains ownership of all proprietary methodologies, frameworks, and tools developed prior to or independent of this engagement. Client receives license to use frameworks developed during engagement for internal purposes only."</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Why it matters:** Your frameworks are your intellectual capital — the asset that justifies premium rates. If clients own your methodology, they can implement it without you. Keep your IP; license the application.</SbCardText>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

</SbSection>

<SbSection id="manipulations">

<SbHeading>Common Manipulations</SbHeading>

Five tactics that extract advisory value without paying for it. Each one is common, predictable, and preventable.

<SbCards>
  <SbCard label="The 'Free Consulting' Discovery Call" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Extensive "exploratory meetings" where the client extracts strategic advice under the guise of evaluating partnership. Two or three "let's dig deeper" calls later, they have your strategy — and never formalize an engagement.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** They were never evaluating you. They were getting free consulting from someone willing to audition. The decision was already made before the first call.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>I'm happy to have one exploratory conversation to assess fit. Beyond that, deeper strategic discussions happen within a paid engagement. I don't provide strategic recommendations outside advisory relationships — it wouldn't be fair to my paying clients.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="The 'Trial Period' at Reduced Rates" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Client requests 30–60 day "trial" at 50% rates to "see if it works." They get cheap strategic advice, then don't renew — or claim it "didn't work" to extend the discount.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Strategic impact takes months to manifest. A 30-day trial is designed to extract value cheaply, not to evaluate fit. Fit is established in the discovery conversation, not through discounted work.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>I don't do trial periods at reduced rates — my track record speaks for itself. I'm happy to share client references. Strategic advisory impact requires time, which is why I work in 6-month minimum engagements at full rates.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="Scope Creep via 'Quick Questions'" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Constant "quick questions" via email and Slack accumulate into 10–15 hours of unbilled strategic guidance per month on top of your retainer scope. Each one seems small. Together, they double your actual workload.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Death by a thousand questions. The advisory relationship becomes on-call consulting without the on-call rate. Your effective rate drops 50% and the client believes they're within scope.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>I track time across all advisory touchpoints and reconcile monthly. This month included 12 hours outside our agreed scope, valued at $14,400. Let's either adjust the retainer to reflect actual usage or define tighter boundaries on async access.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="The 'Just Implement It Too' Drift" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** "Your recommendations are great — can you just implement them for us?" The client wants execution work at advisory rates, or free execution on top of advisory fees. Once you start implementing, you're back to selling hours.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** Advisory and execution are different services at different price points. Implementing your own strategy undermines your advisory positioning and traps you back in the execution cycle.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>Implementation is execution work, not advisory. I can recommend excellent execution partners who work within my frameworks, or we can discuss a separate implementation engagement with different pricing. Advisory and execution are distinct services.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
  <SbCard label="The 'Validate My Decision' Engagement" warn="true">
    <SbCardText>**How it works:** Client has already made the decision. They hire you to rubber-stamp it and provide political cover internally. When you offer genuine strategic pushback, they dismiss it — they only wanted your name attached to their plan.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardText>**Reality:** This isn't advisory — it's reputation rental. Your credibility is being used to justify a decision you didn't make, and when it fails, your name is still on it.</SbCardText>
    <SbCardSub>Sample Response</SbCardSub>
    <SbScript>My value is in providing candid strategic guidance, even when it challenges assumptions. If you're looking for validation of existing plans, I might not be the right advisor. I work with clients who want genuine strategic thinking, not political cover.</SbScript>
  </SbCard>
</SbCards>

<SbSection id="negotiate">

<SbHeading>Negotiation</SbHeading>

Your leverage determines your strategy. Three approaches based on how urgently the client needs strategic guidance and how rare your specific expertise is.

<SbNeg tabsJson='["High Leverage", "Moderate", "Low Leverage"]'>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Maintain premium rates, no discounting.** Quote retainer at full rate with favorable terms — equity component, success fees, board observer seat. "My advisory retainer is $40K/month with a 6-month minimum. I'm also open to equity alignment."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Require C-suite access and clear mandate.** Push for board advisor seat ($50–75K/year + 0.25–0.5% equity) in addition to retainer. Add success fees tied to strategic outcomes you influence.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Client facing urgent, high-stakes decision.** You have unique expertise, referral from trusted source, competitive demand from other clients, and documented ROI from previous advisory work.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Offer hybrid compensation to reduce cash commitment.** "I can structure this as 60% cash retainer + equity alignment, or base retainer + success fee. This reduces your cash outlay and aligns our incentives."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Lock in scope definition and minimum term.** 6-month minimum, clear boundaries between advisory and execution, 60-day termination notice. These protections matter more than the base number.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**Client is interested but comparing options.** Multiple qualified advisors exist, but your domain expertise is relevant. Budget exists but isn't unlimited.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
  <SbNegPanel>
    <SbNegRow label="Position">**Start with project-based engagement** instead of retainer. "Let's begin with a strategic audit — $25K for a 3-week assessment. If it delivers value, we discuss ongoing advisory."</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Add">**Build case studies with generous documentation rights.** Focus on proving outcomes, not maximizing rate. This engagement is an investment in your advisory positioning.</SbNegRow>
    <SbNegRow label="Signal">**You're establishing advisory practice** or entering a new domain. The client is a credential-building opportunity. Don't discount — restructure the engagement to lower commitment.</SbNegRow>
  </SbNegPanel>
</SbNeg>

<SbSection id="value">

<SbHeading>Example Value Calculation: Elena's Advisory Transition</SbHeading>

Elena is a brand strategist with 18 years of experience, earning $180K annually doing brand identity projects at $25–40K each. She works 1,800+ hours/year. Clients increasingly ask her strategic opinion — and she realizes her 4-hour strategy sessions create more value than her 150-hour executions.

<SbChart title="Year 2 Annual Revenue — Advisory vs. Execution Model">
  <SbChartRow label="Advisory (5 clients, ~900 hrs/yr)" value="$1.05M" pct="100" />
  <SbChartRow label="Execution (8 projects, ~1,800 hrs/yr)" value="$180K" pct="17" />
</SbChart>

**Without the structure:** Elena continues trading 1,800 hours/year for $180K. Each project averages $120/hour effective. She's exhausted, creatively drained, and her income is capped by her capacity to execute.

**With the structure:** Elena transitions 5 clients to advisory retainers at $20–30K/month. She works ~900 hours/year — half the time — for **$1.05M** including 3 equity positions worth ~$600K on paper. Her effective rate: **$1,167/hour.** She has time for speaking ($75K), board seats ($150K), and thought leadership that generates more advisory inbound.

**What changed:** Elena stopped selling brand identities and started selling brand judgment. Same expertise, same clients, same relationships — but structured as advisory rather than execution. The 4 hours of strategy that were always the real value became the entire engagement.

<SbSection id="cases">

<SbHeading>Example Use Cases</SbHeading>

How three creatives at different career stages made the transition from execution to advisory — and what happened.

<SbCase label="Case 01 · Consumer Brands · Established" name="Victor Hale — Creative Strategy Advisor" tag="Consumer / DTC" outcome="$1.51M in 18 months">
  <SbCaseText>Victor spent 12 years as Creative Director at top agencies, leading campaigns for major consumer brands. He was earning $250K in salary working 60–70 hour weeks. His breaking point: a DTC founder told him "that 2-hour strategy session was more valuable than the entire $500K campaign we ran with your agency." He realized his judgment was more valuable than his execution.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** He repositioned from "Creative Director" to "Creative Strategy Advisor for High-Growth Consumer Brands." Reached out to 20 previous startup clients offering advisory instead of campaign work. Within 4 months, he had 3 retainer clients at **$15–20K/month** — already $564K annualized. By month 18: 7 active clients, **$1.51M annual revenue** working 30–35 hours/week, plus equity positions in 5 companies worth $2–3M on paper.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** His VC relationships from agency work became a referral engine — one VC partner referred 3 portfolio companies in 6 months. Advisory positioning attracted higher-caliber clients than agency work ever did, because he was selecting for strategic fit rather than taking any project that paid.</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 02 · SaaS / Tech · Mid-Career" name="Anika Mehta — Technical Strategy Advisor" tag="SaaS / Tech" outcome="$1.95M in Year 1">
  <SbCaseText>Anika, a senior full-stack developer with 10 years experience, was earning $200K at a major tech company plus $75K in side consulting at $150/hour. A CTO she'd built a microservices architecture for told her: "The 2-hour conversation about system architecture saved us from a $500K rebuild. That conversation was worth more than all the code you wrote."</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** She developed a "Technical Strategy Advisory" service with three tiers: Strategic Advisory ($25K/month), Board Technical Advisor ($50K/month + equity), and project-based engagements ($40–75K). Within 8 months, she had 5 clients at **$135K/month combined** — and quit her full-time job. Year 1 total: **$1.95M** including retainers, projects, and her partial-year salary. Working 60 hours/month instead of 240+.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** She overcame impostor syndrome by documenting client outcomes: one client saved $2M in infrastructure costs from her architecture recommendations, another raised Series C with "technical excellence" cited by investors. The proof was in what she prevented, not what she produced.</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>

<SbCase label="Case 03 · Communications · Emerging" name="Nora Calloway — Strategic Comms Advisor" tag="Healthcare / Fintech" outcome="$950K in Year 1">
  <SbCaseText>Nora, a veteran journalist turned content strategist, was earning $175K at $200/hour through content production. She developed messaging strategy for a healthcare startup — 20 hours of work, $4,000 — that became the foundation for their $45M Series B. She'd created the strategic foundation for a $45M raise and captured 0.009% of that value.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**The shift:** She repositioned as "Strategic Communications Advisor for High-Stakes Business Moments" — fundraising, M&A, crisis, major launches. Her first advisory client: the same startup, now at **$35K/month** for fundraising narrative coaching (no writing — pure strategic guidance). By month 12: 4 retainer clients plus 4 high-stakes project engagements. **Year 1: $950K** working 700 hours instead of 1,500+.</SbCaseText>
  <SbCaseText>**Key insight:** High-stakes positioning was the decisive move. "Strategic communications" is vague. "The person you call when $45M is on the line" is specific, urgent, and justifies premium rates. Her journalist credibility — 20 years at top publications — gave clients confidence that few "consultants" could match.</SbCaseText>
</SbCase>
